# Topic-Coverage Pass — full 11+ maths syllabus vs our set (Babysitter, 2026-06-22)

**Why:** owner asked, before finalising T219, to "make sure there's nothing else we're missing from
the set." This is the DEEP pass: our **32 built** + **7 T219-planned** topics diffed against the
COMPLETE 11+ maths syllabus (GL, CEM/Durham, ISEB, Bond, Atom, CGP + topic-index aggregators), every
candidate gap judged against our **numpad constraints** (single unambiguous numeric answer; integer or
clean terminating decimal; **no minus key**; no MCQ/text/drawing/plotting/diagram-reading). Method: a
research sub-agent built the provider-corroborated master domain list, then judged each domain
covered? / drillable? / recommendation. Sources at the foot.

## Result in one line
T219's 7 (BODMAS, ×-tricks, primes, Roman→number, negatives-P1, algebra/function-machines, roots) are
**confirmed in-scope and correct** — but the pass found **8 further high-frequency, cleanly-drillable
gaps** (geometry/measurement + one-directional families we only do half of), plus a tier of
nice-to-haves, plus a clearly-bounded set of **format-blocked** domains that are correct deliberate
omissions (not oversights).

## A. CLEARLY SHOULD ADD — high-frequency, core 11+, clean numeric fit (NOT yet in T219)
Each is a single positive numeric answer once the dims/values are stated in text, so it fits the
mental-drill format. Group + tier noted.

1. **Area & Perimeter** — rectangle area, triangle area (½·b·h), perimeter; dims in the prompt.
   *Measures · Tier 3.* Universally tested; trivially numpad-safe.
2. **Volume (cuboid)** — l×w×h. *Measures · Tier 2–3.* Pure mental multiplication; pairs with Area.
3. **Angles — find the missing angle** — on a line (180), around a point (360), triangle sum (180),
   simple parallel-line cases. *Geometry (new group) · Tier 3.* Answer = one positive integer.
4. **Speed–Distance–Time** — D=S×T and its rearrangements, clean numbers. *Measures · Tier 4.* The most
   DEBATABLE for a 10–11 mental drill (higher-tier boards: CSSE/SET); classic formula recall.
5. **Median · Mode · Range** — extend the `mean` family (we only do mean). *Reasoning · Tier 3.* Closes
   the "averages" set; all single-integer answers.
6. **F↔D↔P conversions** — %→decimal, decimal→%, fraction→% (⅗→60). *Fractions & % · Tier 2.* We only do
   F→D today; providers call three-way conversion "a common requirement."
7. **Percentage INCREASE (find the new total)** — 200 +15% → 230. *Fractions & % · Tier 3.* `percentoff`
   only does the decrease-remainder direction; increase is a genuine one-way gap.
8. **Prime factorisation / factors & multiples** — "# of factors of N", "next multiple", "product of
   prime factors". *Number · Tier 3.* `lcmhcf`+planned-`primes` don't cover the factor-count/factorise
   mechanic.

## B. NICE-TO-HAVE — in-scope + drillable but secondary (owner's call)
9. **Indices / powers** — 2^4, 10^3 (extends squares/cubes). *Tier 3.*
10. **Fraction equivalence / simplification — integer-answer form** — "⅗ = ?/20 → 12", "simplify 12/16
    → ?/4 → 3". Makes a format-blocked domain drillable. *Tier 3.*
11. **Probability (single event → clean decimal)** — 3/10 → 0.3. *Tier 3.*
12. **24-hour / am–pm conversion** — express as minutes-since-midnight or the 24h number. *Tier 2.*
13. **Rounding to 1 dp / 1 sf** — extend `rounding`. *Tier 2.*
14. **2D/3D shape property counts** — faces/edges/vertices of a NAMED solid (numeric). *Tier 2.*
15. **"Express as a %"** — "30 is what % of 120 → 25" (reverse-% as a percent). *Tier 3.*

## C. FORMAT-BLOCKED — genuinely 11+-tested but NOT drillable on our numpad (deliberate omissions)
Correct to omit; the underlying arithmetic each relies on is already drilled atomically elsewhere.
- **Coordinates & plotting** (midpoint is a marginal numeric exception).
- **Transformations** (reflect/rotate/translate — visual/coordinate output).
- **Nets** of 3D shapes (visual identification/drawing).
- **Symmetry** (needs a shape image; only "lines of symmetry of a *named* regular polygon" is numeric — marginal).
- **Data handling / chart & graph reading** (bar/line/pie, pictogram, Venn — needs an image).
- **Reading scales / dials / number lines** (visual scale).
- **Timetables** (needs the timetable image; the duration maths IS covered by `timegap`).
- **Multi-sentence worded problems with diagrams** (out of the mental-drill format by design).
- **Ruler/protractor drawing & measuring** (physical-tool tasks).
- **Negative-ANSWER subtraction** (e.g. 5−8, temperature *fall* below zero) — blocked by the no-minus-key
  rule; the planned **negatives-P1** (non-negative answers only) is the correct compromise.
